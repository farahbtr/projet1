introduction

Radiotherapy means the use of radiation, usually x-rays, to treat cancer. You might have radiotherapy from inside the body, called internal radiotherapy. Or external radiotherapy, which is from outside the body. Radiotherapy can be used to try to cure cancer, reduce the chance of cancer coming back or to help relieve symptoms. You might have it by itself or with other treatments, such as chemotherapy or surgery.

when radiotherapy is used

Radiotherapy may be used in the early stages of cancer or after it has started to spread. It can be used to:

  1. try to cure the cancer completely (curative radiotherapy)
  2. make other treatments more effective â€“ for example, it can be combined with chemotherapy or used before surgery (neo-adjuvant radiotherapy)
  3. reduce the risk of the cancer coming back after surgery (adjuvant radiotherapy)
  4. relieve symptoms if a cure is not possible (palliative radiotherapy)

side effects

  • external radiotherapy, where a machine is used to carefully aim beams of radiation at the cancer
  • radiotherapy implants (brachytherapy), where small pieces of radioactive metal are (usually temporarily) placed inside your body near the cancer
  • radiotherapy injections, capsules or drinks (radioisotope therapy), where you swallow a radioactive liquid, or have it injected into your blood
